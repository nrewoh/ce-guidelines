# Pain Assessment & Management (Updated in June 2022)

> 💡 **CLINICAL PEARL:** Accurate pain assessment is essential to prompt and humane pediatric emergency care. Pain should be treated alongside diagnostic workups, not delayed by them.

## 1. Age-Appropriate Pain Assessment Scales
Select the appropriate tool based on the child's developmental age:

| Age Group | Assessment Tool | Indicators Evaluated |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< 4 years (or non-verbal)** | **FLACC Scale** | Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability |
| **4 to 7 years** | **Wong-Baker FACES** | Self-reported scale using 6 visual faces (0–10) |
| **> 7 years (verbal)** | **Visual Analogue / Numeric** | Verbal rating from 0 (no pain) to 10 (worst pain) |

## 2. First-Line Pharmacological Interventions
Always check weights carefully before administering pediatric dosages.

* **Mild Pain (Score 1–3):** 
  * *Paracetamol:* 15 mg/kg oral or rectal (Max 1g per dose)
* **Moderate Pain (Score 4–6):** 
  * *Ibuprofen:* 10 mg/kg oral (Max 400mg per dose; avoid if dehydrated or suspected bleeding)
* **Severe Pain (Score 7–10):** 
  * Initiate immediate medical review. Consider intranasal fentanyl or intravenous morphine as per local emergency protocol.

## 3. Non-Pharmacological Strategies
* **Infants:** Sucrose solution (24%), swaddling, non-nutritive sucking.
* **Toddlers/Older Children:** Distraction techniques (bubbles, videos), cold sprays/packs for localized injuries, and parental presence.
