# Triage Principles (Updated in June 2024)

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL REQUIREMENT:** Triage must be rapid, focused, and performed by a designated, trained emergency nurse immediately upon patient arrival.

## 1. Core Principles
* **Clinical Urgency First:** Patients are prioritized strictly by clinical urgency, completely independent of social status, arrival order, or financial status.
* **Dynamic Assessment:** Triage is a continuous process. A patient's condition can deteriorate while waiting, requiring regular reassessments.
* **Safety First:** If a child looks critically unwell at first glance, skip formal documentation and move them straight to the Resuscitation room immediately.

## 2. The Triage Process Flow
1. **Visual Sweep:** Initial "across-the-room" look using the Pediatric Assessment Triangle (PAT).
2. **Focused History:** Main presenting complaint, duration, and key associated symptoms.
3. **Objective Data:** Vital signs (HR, RR, SpO2, Temperature) and pain score.
4. **Acuity Assignment:** Assigning the appropriate Patient Acuity Category Scale (PACS) tier.

## 3. Re-Triage Protocols
Patients waiting in the waiting area must be re-evaluated according to their initial category or sooner if parents report changes:
* **PACS 2:** Every 15 minutes
* **PACS 3:** Every 60 minutes
