# Patient Acuity Category Scale (Updated in June 2022)

The **Patient Acuity Category Scale (PACS)** dictates patient assignment and target physician evaluation times within the Children's Emergency Department.

| Category | Definition | Target Target Evaluation Time | Typical Presentations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PACS 1** | **Resuscitation** | Immediate / Continuous | Cardiac/respiratory arrest, status epilepticus, severe shock, anaphylaxis, severe trauma. |
| **PACS 2** | **Major Emergency** | Within 15 minutes | Severe respiratory distress, altered mental state, status asthmaticus, high-risk poisoning, severe pain. |
| **PACS 3** | **Minor Emergency** | Within 60 minutes | Stable fractures, mild dehydration, moderate asthma, persistent vomiting without severe fluid loss. |
| **PACS 4** | **Non-Emergency** | > 120 minutes | Chronic symptoms, mild upper respiratory tract infections, superficial grazes, diaper rashes. |

## Vital Sign Trigger Adjustments
If a child's objective vital signs cross critical physiological thresholds (severe tachycardia, bradycardia, or hypoxia), their triage status must automatically be up-triaged to a higher acuity level regardless of presenting complaints.
